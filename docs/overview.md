# Project Overview

ExpertNetworks.net is an independent research platform that helps professional buyers evaluate, compare, and understand expert network providers.

## What It Is

A static website that organizes publicly available information about the expert network industry into a structured, navigable, and comparable format. The site covers 33 providers across dimensions that matter to institutional buyers: pricing, compliance posture, AI capabilities, delivery model, transcript access, scale, and market positioning.

## What Problem It Solves

The expert network market is a $2B+ industry serving consulting firms, private equity, hedge funds, and enterprise strategy teams. Despite its size, public information about individual providers is fragmented, inconsistent, and often promotional.

A buyer trying to answer basic questions — *Who are the major providers? How much do they cost? What compliance frameworks do they follow? Which ones offer AI-assisted matching?* — would need to visit dozens of websites, read through marketing copy, and piece together a picture that no single source provides.

This project consolidates that fragmented information into one structured resource.

## What Kind of Product It Is

This is an **information product**, not a software platform. It does not broker expert calls, generate leads, or sell subscriptions. It is a research tool — closer in spirit to a well-maintained industry guide than to a SaaS application.

The technical implementation (Astro static site, JSON content model, TypeScript validation) serves the content. The content is the product.

## What Differentiates It

- **Independence.** No paid placements, no affiliate arrangements, no provider partnerships.
- **Structure.** Provider data is not free-form prose — it is structured, filterable, and comparable across 29 dimensions.
- **Confidence labeling.** Claims are tagged per-field as verified, positioned, inferred, or estimated. This is unusual for directory-style sites.
- **Buyer orientation.** The site is organized around buyer workflows (by industry, by use case, by decision stage) rather than just provider listings.
- **Public-source methodology.** The research approach and its limitations are documented on the site, not hidden.

## Scale

- 33 expert network providers tracked
- 57 industry intelligence signals
- 58 static pages (including 33 dynamic provider profiles)
- 29 structured comparison dimensions
- 14 comparison presets by use case
- 7 pricing models documented
- 6 head-to-head comparison pages
- 3 provider alternatives pages
- 3 industry-specific buyer guides
- 8,800+ internal links validated at build time
