# Methodology and Editorial Standards

ExpertNetworks.net is an independent research product built on public-source information. This document explains the standards behind the site's content and the reasoning for each.

## Research Approach

### Public-Source Only

All provider information on the site is drawn from publicly available materials:

- **Primary sources:** Provider websites, regulatory filings, public financial disclosures, patent filings, company registrations
- **Independent reporting:** Journalism (trade press, business media), analyst coverage, industry association publications
- **Company-originated content:** Press releases, blog posts, marketing materials, published case studies
- **Editorial synthesis:** Comparative analysis, market positioning assessments, and inferred conclusions drawn from the above

No information is obtained through private conversations, insider sources, or confidential materials. This constraint limits depth in some areas — particularly pricing and internal compliance practices — but it ensures that every claim on the site can be traced to a public reference.

### No Paid Placements

No provider has paid for positioning, favorable coverage, or priority placement on any page. Rankings, comparisons, and featured selections are based on editorial judgment, not commercial arrangements. This applies to the directory, comparison presets, pricing guide, and all recommendation pages.

### No Affiliate Arrangements

The site does not generate revenue through referral links, affiliate fees, or lead-generation agreements. Provider links point to official provider websites without tracking parameters.

## Confidence Labeling

Not all public information is equally reliable. A claim on a provider's website about their own capabilities is different from an independent audit or a regulatory filing. The site's confidence labeling system makes this distinction explicit.

### Label Definitions

- **Verified** — Confirmed by the provider and independently corroborated, or sourced from a regulatory filing, financial disclosure, or credible third-party report.
- **Positioned** — Stated by the provider in marketing materials or press releases. Not independently verified, but the source is identified.
- **Inferred** — Based on third-party benchmarks, market reporting, or patterns observed across multiple sources. The reasoning is documented.
- **Estimated** — Directional only. Based on incomplete data, extrapolation, or limited sources. Used sparingly and flagged clearly.

### Per-Field Application

Confidence labels are applied per data field, not per provider profile. A single provider's profile may contain verified founding dates, positioned service descriptions, inferred pricing ranges, and estimated expert counts. This granularity helps users calibrate their trust in each piece of information independently.

## Editorial Principles

### Accuracy Over Speed

Information is added to the site after verification against available sources, not in response to press cycles or breaking news. The news feed tracks industry developments, but provider profiles are updated on an editorial schedule rather than reactively.

### Evidence Over Assertion

The site describes what providers offer, how they position themselves, and what independent sources report. It avoids conclusory statements that cannot be supported by evidence ("the best compliance program in the industry") in favor of specific, attributable claims ("ISO 27001 certified as of January 2026, per company announcement").

### Careful Language Around Rankings

The "Best Expert Networks" page and comparison presets involve editorial judgment. These are framed explicitly as interpretation, not objective measurement. The methodology behind rankings (scope, compliance maturity, AI investment, market presence) is disclosed on the page.

### Distinction Between Types of Claims

The site distinguishes between:

- **Factual claims** — founding dates, headquarters locations, certifications (verifiable)
- **Provider claims** — "700+ industries covered," "1M+ experts" (positioned, not independently verified)
- **Editorial assessments** — "strong compliance posture," "well-suited for PE workflows" (inferred from available evidence)

This distinction is reflected in both the confidence labels and the editorial tone.

## Known Limitations

### Coverage Is Not Comprehensive

The directory tracks 33 providers. This is a curated and expanding subset of the market, not a complete census. Smaller, regional, or highly specialized networks may not yet be included. Absence from the directory does not imply a negative assessment.

### Provider Data Varies in Depth

Some providers publish extensive information. Others operate with minimal public disclosure. Profile depth reflects the available public record, not editorial preference. Providers with richer public documentation will naturally have more detailed profiles.

### Expert Counts Are Directional

"Expert count" figures (e.g., "1.2M network members") are sourced from provider marketing and should be treated as directional rather than precise. Definitions vary across providers — some count all registered profiles, others count only vetted or active experts.

### Pricing Is Approximate

Published pricing ranges reflect market reporting, third-party benchmarks (notably Inex One), and provider disclosures where available. Actual pricing is typically negotiated and varies by engagement scope, volume, and relationship.

### Currency of Information

All information reflects the public record at the time of the most recent update. The expert network industry moves quickly. The site's `lastUpdated` field on provider profiles and datestamps on news items help users assess currency.

## Corrections

When errors are identified — whether through user feedback, new public information, or internal review — they are corrected promptly. Material corrections to provider profiles are noted in the commit history.

The site does not maintain a formal errata page, but the research approach prioritizes accuracy over comprehensiveness.
